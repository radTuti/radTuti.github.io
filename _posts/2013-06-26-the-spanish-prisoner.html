I recently read 419 by William Ferguson which is about the Nigerian Scam popularly called 419*. A friend asked me to do a review when I tweeted a picture of my new purchases (which included the book 419).

I was elated by the accurate portrayal of the Nigerian Airport and how the Nigerian weather might feel to someone coming from Canada. In the beginning, I somewhat forced myself to keep reading. While I recognized that Laura was the protagonist, I was mostly interested in Nnamdi's story which hurt me what he went through in the end.

I cannot for the life of me place why Amina was in the story as there wasn't much background, all I figured was she once has a better heritage but now she has to keep heading south though her people were from the North (presumably outside Nigeria and associated with Hausas). I do not know if this was deliberate but I appreciate the author's attempt to capture the three "major"Â parts of Nigeria (Hausa, Igbo & Yoruba) as Kaduna, Niger Delta & Lagos - now we can digress and get critical about how people from Niger Delta aren't Igbo.

Ignorant as I am still - I am vaguely aware of the history of Niger Delta - I was impressed by the history given to Nnamdi. While I admit that a few other things didn't make sense or felt irrelevant, I'll have to say it was a good read. Readers are able to sympathize with the major characters - albeit to vary degrees - and their situation. One thing I'll like further explanation is how Laura defied the odds and what she did in the end.

I cannot rate the book on a scale and my review is mostly bland because I was reading it concurrently with Quiet: The Power of Introverts by Susan Cain which I took a hiatus from to read Z: A novel of Zelda Fitzgerald by Therese Anne Fowler.

After finishing Z, I've the need to read all of F. Scott Fitzgerald's short stories. In addition, due to my fascination with the Twenties I want to read The Great Gatsby - I've never read it - especially since I'm finally planning the party I should have had for my last birthday for the upcoming birthday.

* 419 is a Nigerian adaptation of the Spanish Prisoner scam with the use of modern communication technologies.
